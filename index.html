About Me

I am a 5th year graduate student in astronomy at New Mexico State University working with machine learning, asterosiesmology, stellar evolution, and galatic archeology .

My research focus is on leveraging various machine learning techniques to study large astronomical datasets, such as spectroscopy from the Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) MWM program, asterosiesmology from the Kepler and TESS missions, and astrometry from Gaia. In particular, I am interested in determining stellar ages from chemical abundances, calibrated with Asterosiesmology, and then applied with SDSS and Gaia data to study galactic archaeology.

I love sharing what I know with other both in and out of the classroom. I enjoy doing both astronomy and general science outreach, and have even begun trying to pioneer using VR and other tools to facilitate new outreach possiabilties. Reach out if you have any questions or would like to collaborate on VR outreach prospects

I also love learning new things, such as operating amature radio, building stuff in the garage, playing music, 3D modelling, and recently trying to learn new languages. I have a particulary deep love of flying and hope to finish my private pilot's license this year.
